Title: Introduction to the Course
Course: Applied Combinatorics - MATH-3012-K&L
Content:
Welcome to Math 3012! Your instructor is Dr. Changxin Ding. This course is all about counting- but not just in the way you learned as a child. We want to explore the mathematical structures at work behind the scenes and use them to solve harder problems. In addition to learning specific knowledge and tools, we have some overall goals to work towards- by the end of the semester, we hope you’ll be able to: Apply basic counting techniques, and combine them to solve more complex problems Prove statements at the appropriate level using the principles of induction, inclusion/exclusion,and other combinatorial methods Solve problems at an appropriate level using recurrence relations, generating functions, andtools and ideas from graph theory In the above areas, communicate and document your mathematical reasoning and processeffectively Navigation Instructions The following tabs should show up in the navigation bar to the right, which you can use to move around our site. The Announcements tab takes you to the course announcements, where I will post course related information throughout the semester. I recommend checking your Canvas settings to be sure you also get notified when an announcement is posted. The Modules tab takes you to the coursework. The Ed Discussion tab will take you to our Ed Discussion page, where you can find discussion/question boards to help you stay connected asynchronously. Please review and take advantage of these resources as necessary, and feel free to reach out if you need help connecting with any other assistance.
